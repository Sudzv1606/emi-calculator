EMI Calculation Formula

Published on February 27, 2025

The Equated Monthly Installment (EMI) calculation formula is a fundamental tool for borrowers in the US and Canada to determine fixed monthly loan repayments. It combines principal and interest into a single payment, ensuring predictability for home loans, auto loans, and personal loans. This formula, widely used in North America, helps borrowers in Toronto, New York, or Vancouver plan finances effectively, and our free EMI calculator simplifies its application.

EMI originated from early 20th-century lending practices, becoming standard with US FHA mortgages and Canadian CMHC programs. In 2024, the Federal Reserve reported over $17 trillion in US consumer debt uses this formula, while the Bank of Canada noted CAD 2.2 trillion in Canadian loans, underscoring its importance for borrowers in California, Alberta, and beyond.

The EMI Formula Explained

The EMI formula is: EMI = P × r × (1 + r)^n / [(1 + r)^n - 1], where:

  • P = Principal loan amount (e.g., $100,000 for a mortgage).
  • r = Monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100, e.g., 6% = 0.06 ÷ 12 = 0.005).
  • n = Number of monthly payments (tenure in years × 12, e.g., 30 years = 360 months).

This formula uses the reducing balance method, calculating interest only on the outstanding principal, ensuring efficiency for long-term loans in both countries.

Practical Examples for US and Canada

Consider these scenarios based on 2025 averages from Freddie Mac and CMHC:

  • US Home Loan: $300,000 at 6% over 30 years:
    • r = 0.06 ÷ 12 = 0.005
    • n = 360
    • EMI = [300000 × 0.005 × (1 + 0.005)^360] / [(1 + 0.005)^360 - 1] ≈ $1,798.65
    • Total Interest ≈ $247,511.00; Total Paid ≈ $547,511.00
  • Canadian Mortgage: CAD 400,000 at 5.5% over 25 years:
    • r = 0.055 ÷ 12 ≈ 0.004583
    • n = 300
    • EMI ≈ CAD 2,334.44
    • Total Interest ≈ CAD 300,332.00; Total Paid ≈ CAD 700,332.00
