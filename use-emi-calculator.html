How to Use an EMI Calculator

Input three variables: principal (P), interest rate (r), and tenure (n) into the formula EMI = P × r × (1 + r)^n / [(1 + r)^n - 1]. Our calculator handles this automatically, providing:

  • Monthly EMI, total interest, and repayment for a $300,000 US home loan at 6% over 30 years: EMI ≈ $1,798.65, interest ≈ $247,511.00, total ≈ $547,511.00.
  • Monthly EMI, total interest, and repayment for a CAD 400,000 Canadian mortgage at 5.5% over 25 years: EMI ≈ CAD 2,334.44, interest ≈ CAD 300,332.00, total ≈ CAD 700,332.00.

Use it to test rate changes, tenures, or extra payments, per Freddie Mac and CMHC’s 2025 guidelines for US and Canadian borrowers.
