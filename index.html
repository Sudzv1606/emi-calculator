Understanding Your Loan

  • EMI (Equated Monthly Installment): The fixed amount you pay each month to repay your loan.
  • Annual Interest Rate: The yearly rate charged on your loan, expressed as a percentage.
  • Loan Tenure: The duration over which you will repay the loan, in years.
  • Extra Payments: Additional amounts you can pay to reduce your loan balance faster.

How to Use the Calculator

For example, if you have a loan of $10,000 at an annual interest rate of 5% for 2 years, with an extra payment of $100 every 6 months, the calculator will show your EMI, a detailed amortization schedule, and a chart of your remaining balance over time.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an amortization schedule?

An amortization schedule shows how each payment is split between principal and interest, and how the loan balance decreases over time.

Can I use this calculator for different loan types?

Yes, this calculator can be used for various loan types, including personal loans, car loans, and mortgages in the US and Canada.

How do extra payments affect my loan?

Extra payments reduce your loan balance faster, potentially shortening your loan term and saving you interest.
