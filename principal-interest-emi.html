Principal, Interest & EMI

Published on February 27, 2025

Principal, interest, and Equated Monthly Installment (EMI) are the core components of loan repayment for borrowers in the US and Canada. The principal is the borrowed amount, interest is the cost of borrowing, and EMI is the fixed monthly payment combining both. Understanding Principal, Interest, and EMI

Principal: The original loan amount (e.g., $100,000 for a mortgage) reduced with each payment. Interest: The cost charged by lenders, calculated on the remaining principal using the reducing balance method (e.g., 6% annually). EMI: A fixed payment combining both, calculated as EMI = P × r × (1 + r)^n / [(1 + r)^n - 1].

Early EMI payments are interest-heavy, but as principal decreases, interest shrinks, and principal repayment grows, ensuring the loan is paid off by the end of the tenure.
